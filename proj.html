Semiconductor Refrigeration System


Thermoelectric Refrigeration


In 1834 Jean Peltier noted that when an electrical current is applied across the junction of two dissimilar metals, heat is removed from one of the metals and transferred to the other. On the basis of this ,we came to an end with this thermoelectric refrigeration system. Here in the figure you can see a picture of thermoelectric module that is used in respective project.

Theme


The basic idea of this system is to conserve electricity and an eco-friendly equipment. In many cases, people cannot afford a regular size refrigerator. Especially students who are staying far from their home can neither afford nor carry such a big appliance along with them. We can also know the weight in the fridge and check whether it is overloaded or not.

This whole mechanism consumes about 60Watts power and cost approximately not more than Rs1500/- Not only this we can also control and monitor the temperature and refrigeration.



Working


The circuit is built around microcontroller ATmega8, Temperature sensor LM 35, thermoelectric module TEC1-12706, desktop computer’s SMPS, DC fan, seven segment display, and relay.
The complete circuit is powered by desktop computer’s SMPS. 12V and 5V outputs from the SMPS are connected to CON1. 5V of power is needed by the microcontroller and rest of the circuit, while 12V is used for DC fan and the thermoelectric module.
